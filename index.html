The White Borders Problem

When a design is printed without bleed margin, minor shifts during the cutting process can leave unwanted white edges on your final product. This ruins the professional aesthetics of your printed materials.

What is the Bleed Margin?

The bleed margin is the area extending beyond the edge of your design. This extra space (typically 0.25 inches) is trimmed during production to ensure your design reaches the paper's edge without white gaps.


Minor shifts during cutting can leave unsightly white edges on printed materials, ruining your design's professional appearance.


You can visualize and configure these margins with pixel-perfect precision, guaranteeing your designs always print flawlessly.

The Precision Magic of Canvas

Canvas enables you to visualize and configure bleed margins with absolute precision. Our interactive tool shows exactly how your design will appear after the cutting process.

Step-by-Step Guide

1

Set Up Your Document

Set your final design size and add an extra 0.25 inches on each side for the bleed area.

2

Extend your elements

Ensure background elements and images extend to the edge of the bleed area.

3

Keep content within the safe zone

Place all important content at least 0.125 inches inside the trim edge to avoid cutting it off.

4

Export with printer's marks

When exporting your design, include crop marks and bleed to guide the printer.
